## Why Elno wins

Let's be blunt about the competition.

### vs X / Twitter

X owns your account. You're renting it, and the landlord reads your mail.

- They hold your identity in their database. They can ban you overnight, and there's no appeal that means anything.
- They sell your data and run an algorithm *against* you — engineered to keep you scrolling, angry, and watching ads.
- On X, **you are the product.**

On Elno, you own your posts outright. They're anonymous, uncensorable, and the economic plan flips the whole deal: **the network is built to pay you instead of selling you.**

### vs Farcaster, Lens, Nostr, Bluesky

Most "decentralized" social isn't, all the way down.

- **Farcaster / Lens** keep your posts off-chain — in hubs, in indexers — because putting everything on a chain was assumed to be too expensive. The chain holds a pointer, not the post.
- **Bluesky** is federated servers (PDS + relays). Better than X, but it's still hosts that can defederate, go down, or change the rules.
- **Nostr** is the closest in spirit — key-based, anonymous, no account to ban. We respect it. But Nostr runs on relays, not a verifiable blockchain. There's no economic settlement layer, no on-chain proof, no built-in way to pay users back.

Elno's bet is the one nobody else made: **posting is cheap enough on Solana to put every post on-chain for real** — and then build an economy on top where users are paid.
